About Us

Talent gold plus is digital sport marketplace that connect investors to choose a raw but talented youngster, including women in football and athletics, whom they can invest in with the option to gain a return on their investment once the athlete signs a professional contract – much the same manner as more traditional seed investors.

A safe, supportive and secure environment, helping nurture, mentor young talents and providing them with all the physical, emotional, mental and psychological support they need to be able to succeed.

Support and nurture potential talent from the community, Giving investors the Opportunity to buy into and support the talent’s development whether for profit, or to give back.

Meet Our Olympians

Chioma Ajunwa

First African woman to win an Olympic gold medal in a field event and is the only person to transition from football to athletics and secure an Olympic gold medal.

Dr. Micheal Bruce

Cognitive Psychologist, Elite Speed & Performance coach specializing in Premiership football. Former England International Athlete and Olympic Gold Medal-winning coach.

Ruth Balofin

Bronze medals in snatch, clean and jerk, and overall at the 1999 World Championships (75 kg). Silver medal at the 2000 Summer Olympics (75 kg) with a total lift of 245.0 kg.

Glory Alozie

Competed in hurdling for Nigeria and Spain. she was World junior second placer in 1996 and later became African champion twice, holding records in 100m hurdles.

Stepani Douglas

Silver medal winnner in 4 x 100m relay at 1990 Commonwealth Games, New Zealand, and bronze medal winnner at 1994 Commonwealth Games, Canada.

Marcus Adam

Gold medalist in the 200m and 4x100m relay at 1990 Commonwealth Games, also won silver in 4x100m relay at 1990 European Championships.
