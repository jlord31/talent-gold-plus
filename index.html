Meet Our Ambassador

Who is Chioma Ajunwa?

Chioma Ajunwa hails from a family of nine children—six boys and three girls. As the youngest member of her family, she became the family’s breadwinner through her achievements in sports. The family’s financial struggles meant that when Chioma passed the common entrance exam, there were no funds available for her to attend school. Undeterred, she channeled her energy into sports.

Her determination paid off. Chioma made history as the first African woman to win an Olympic gold medal in a field event. Notably, she is also the only individual—male or female—to have transitioned from football to athletics and secured an Olympic gold medal.

But Chioma’s journey wasn’t without challenges. During her athletics career, few believed in her potential. However, Chief Segun Odegbami stood by her side, ensuring she reached the pinnacle of her career. Inspired by this support, Chioma now aims to give back to society. She wants to help children who lack assistance and be a voice for those who need it. After all, she firmly believes that our attitude in life determines our altitude.
